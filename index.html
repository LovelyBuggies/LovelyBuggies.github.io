Short Bio

I'm a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at Northeastern University, advised by Christopher Amato.

My research interests lie in game theory and reinforcement learning, with focuses on (1) developing cooperative multi-agent reinforcement learning theory and methods in partially observable environments and (2) modeling societal-scale multi-agent system dynamics through game theory to enable scalable control.

Keywords: Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning; Decentralized Partially-Observable Markov Decision Process; Mean-Field Game.
