Short Bio

I am a Ph.D. student at the Khoury College of Computer Sciences at Northeastern University, advised by Christopher Amato. I finished my master’s at Columbia University and undergraduate studies at Sun Yat-sen University, both in CS.

My research focuses on multi-agent reinforcement learning, multi-agent systems, and decentralization. Specifically: (1) Optimizing the LLM collaboration with MARL (CoMLRL); (2) Advancing theory and algorithms for decentralized decision-making in partially observable domains (Dec-POMDP); (3) Developing game-theory approaches for multi-agent planning in societal-scale systems.

Keywords: Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning; Multi-Agent Systems; Large Language Models; Decentralization.
